COCO LUMBER

Coco lumberis derived from mature coconut trees that have reached the end of their fruit-bearing life. It is widely available in the Philippines due to the country's extensive coconut plantations. Coco lumberis an innovative and sustainable alternative to traditional hardwood. Instead of cutting down forest trees, coco lumber makes use of aging coconut palms that would otherwise be discarded or burned. This practice helps reduce deforestation while maximizing the value of existing agricultural resources. The wood varies in density, with the outer portion being extremely hard and durable, while the inner core is softer. When properly treated and processed, coco lumber becomes resistant to pests, moisture, and decay. Its distinctive grain pattern gives it a unique aesthetic that sets it apart from conventional timber. Coco lumberis increasingly used in both rural and urban construction, particularly in affordable housing and eco-conscious architectural projects.

Uses and Application

Housing Construction and Structural Framing

Used in posts, beams, and framing for affordable and eco-friendly homes.

Flooring, Wall Panels, and Ceilings

Provides a natural wood finish with unique grain patterns for interior spaces.

Furniture and Cabinetry

Crafted into tables, chairs, shelves, and cabinets that are sturdy and sustainable.

Doors, Window Frames, and Stairs

Used in residential buildings due to its strength and durability when treated.

Decorative Wood Products

Applied in accents, carvings, and interior design elements.
