    • 5 Types Of Rattan

    1. Natural(Raw) Rattan

    Natural or raw rattan refers to rattan poles that are harvested and minimally processed. At this stage, the rattan retains its original light yellow to pale brown color and natural surface texture. It is flexible when freshly cut and gradually hardens as it dries. Raw rattan is commonly used in traditional furniture-making, basketry, and handicrafts, especially in rural communities. Because it has not yet undergone chemical treatment, this type is more vulnerable to insects and moisture, making it more suitable for indoor use or temporary applications unless further treated.

    2. Treated Rattan

    Treated rattan undergoes processes such as drying, smoking, boiling, or chemical preservation to improve durability and resistance to pests, fungi, and moisture. Treatment significantly extends the lifespan of rattan, making it suitable for long-term use. This type is widely used in commercial furniture, architectural elements, and interior applications. Treated rattan maintains its flexibility while gaining strength and stability, making it ideal for both functional and decorative purposes in homes, resorts, and public spaces.

    3. Skin Rattan (Rattan Peel)

    Skin rattan refers to the outer layer of the rattan cane, which is peeled into thin strips. These strips are smoother and more uniform in appearance compared to the rattan core. Skin rattan is commonly used for weaving furniture surfaces, chair seats, cabinet panels, baskets, and decorative bindings. Its refined texture allows for intricate patterns and clean finishes, making it popular in both traditional and modern designs.

    4. Core Rattan

    Core rattan comes from the inner solid portion of the rattan cane. It is round, strong, and flexible, making it suitable for structural elements. This type is primarily used for furniture frames, supports, armrests, legs, and load-bearing components. Core rattan provides strength while remaining lightweight, allowing furniture to be durable yet easy to move.

    5. Woven or Manufactured Rattan

    Woven or manufactured rattan refers to rattan that has been processed, shaped, and assembled into panels, mats, or furniture components. This includes hand-woven rattan sheets and modular rattan elements. This type is commonly used in room dividers, wall panels, headboards, ceiling accents, and decorative screens, particularly in tropical and contemporary interiors. It showcases craftsmanship while allowing greater design flexibility.
