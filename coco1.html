USES AND APPLICATION

Housing Construction and Structural Framing

Coco lumber is widely used in housing construction, particularly in affordable and sustainable housing projects. It is commonly applied in structural components such as posts, beams, trusses, and framing systems. The high-density outer portion of the coconut trunk provides excellent load-bearing capacity, allowing coco lumber to support roofs and upper floors when properly processed and treated. Its use reduces dependence on traditional hardwood sourced from forests, helping prevent deforestation. In rural and coastal communities, coco lumber is especially valuable because it is locally available, cost-effective, and adaptable to various house designs. When treated against termites and moisture, coco lumber can achieve long service life while maintaining structural integrity.
